Did you know?
Saints were founded in 1885 as St. Mary's Young Men's Association FC — set up by the rector of St. Mary's Church to give young men a healthy alternative to the pub. That direct link to the church is where the nickname "The Saints" comes from.

Heritage May 1976 · 50 years on
The day Bobby Stokes silenced Wembley

"I just hit it. I didn't see it go in. I just heard the crowd. And I knew."

Bobby Stokes interview, 1986 programme
One-club hero Le Tissier · 1986–2002
"I was a Saints fan. Why would I leave?"

Sixteen years, 209 goals, 49 of 50 penalties scored, every Premier League club coming for him — and he stayed, every season. The reason he gave was always the same.

Programme feature, March 2002
Records 2023/24 · under Russell Martin
25 matches without a loss

Following relegation in 2023, the squad ran a 25-match unbeaten streak across all competitions — a club record under Russell Martin's tactical reset. The club rebuilt itself in one season.

Season retrospective, end of 2023/24
Stadium August 2001
From The Dell to St Mary's

103 years at The Dell. The walk over the Itchen on opening day, the queues at the Northam End, a 32,000-seat home for the next 25 seasons — and counting.

Stadium opening programme, 25.08.2001
Records April 2019 · v Watford
Shane Long — 7.69 seconds

From kick-off, Watford passed back. The pass was loose. Long pressed, took it round the keeper, finished on the edge of the box. Fastest goal in Premier League history. The clock said 7.69 seconds.

Match report, programme feature 2019
Heritage South America · summer 1904
The first English club to tour Argentina

Long before international football was a thing, Saints sailed for Buenos Aires and Montevideo to play exhibition matches. The squad helped seed the game across two countries that would later host World Cups.

A first team built from the academy

Bale. Walcott. Oxlade-Chamberlain. Shaw. Ward-Prowse. The line of England internationals that came up through Staplewood reads like a national-team sheet.

Pre-match · your tactics

Locked in at 18:30, fifteen minutes before kick-off. The dressing room got your call.

Pre-match · decision 1

Starting formation

Bristol play through midfield. Saints' XI is fit and rested.

Your call 4-2-3-1 · Larin lone striker

Two holding mids to break Bristol's possession game, three creators behind Larin. Standard Saints shape — what won eight in a row.

Pre-match · decision 2

Match mentality

Eight straight wins on the line. Bristol have form too.

Your call Balanced

Not gung-ho, not parking the bus. Trust the eight wins came from playing the same way you've been playing — don't reinvent the night.

Pre-match · decision 3

Press intensity

90 minutes is a long time to press. Bristol's midfield is patient.

Your call Mid block

Compact in your own half, force Bristol wide, sustainable for the full ninety. Conserves legs for the second-half push.

Pre-match · decision 4

Set-piece routine

Bell's the aerial threat in their box. Wood and Harwood-Bellis aerial-strong in yours.

Your call Defensive: man-mark Bell · Attacking: short corner routine

Bell wins 73% of his aerials — the only way to neutralise him is body-on-body. Saints' best chance from corners is short routines that pull defenders out of position.

First-half result

Play before kick-off

Saints book Hampshire-based bands and solo acts to play the concourse before every home game. Paid slot, kit provided, in front of 31,000 supporters.

What we look for

Original work or covers, 30-minute set, Hampshire-resident or Hampshire-formed acts, public liability insurance. We mostly book singer-songwriters, indie bands and acoustic acts — anything that lifts a concourse.
